# LinkedIn Content Guide

## Format & Length
- **Ideal length:** 150-300 words (1,300 characters is the fold — hook must land before that)
- **Paragraphs:** 1-2 sentences each, separated by blank lines
- **Line breaks matter:** LinkedIn's algorithm favors content that keeps people reading. Short paragraphs = more "see more" clicks

## Structure That Works

### The Hook (Line 1-2)
LinkedIn truncates after ~210 characters. Your opening must earn the click.

**Patterns that stop scrolls:**
- Bold claim: "Most startups don't fail because of bad ideas. They fail because of bad timing."
- Counter-narrative: "I stopped posting daily on LinkedIn. My engagement went up."
- Specific result: "One email template generated $47K in pipeline last quarter."
- Relatable tension: "Your best developer just put in their notice. Now what?"

### The Body
- One core idea, explored from multiple angles
- Use white space aggressively — dense paragraphs get skipped
- Lists work but don't overuse (not every post needs "5 lessons")
- Personal experience adds credibility when it's specific, not when it's vague

### The Close
- End on your strongest point or an open question
- CTAs that work: "What's your take?" / "Have you seen this?" (specific > generic)
- CTAs that don't: "Like if you agree!" / "Share this with your network!"

## Hashtags
- 2-3 maximum, placed at the end
- Mix: 1 broad (#Leadership) + 1-2 niche (#SaaSGrowth, #DevTooling)
- Never put hashtags inline in the text

## Emoji Policy
- Minimal or none in professional contexts
- If used, only as occasional emphasis — not as bullet points
- Never: 🔥🚀💡💪🎯 as paragraph starters

## What the Algorithm Favors (2024-2025)
- Dwell time (long reads)
- Comments over likes
- Posts that spark conversation
- Native content (no external links in the post — put links in comments)
- Document posts (carousels/PDFs) get high reach

## Post Types
1. **Insight post** — Share a non-obvious take on your field
2. **Story post** — A specific experience with a clear takeaway
3. **Framework post** — A mental model or process others can use
4. **Contrarian post** — Challenge conventional wisdom (respectfully)
5. **Data post** — Share numbers, benchmarks, or results
6. **Carousel/Document** — Visual slide deck (suggest creating as PDF)
