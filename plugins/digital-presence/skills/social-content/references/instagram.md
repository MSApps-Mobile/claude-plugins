# Instagram Content Guide

## Format & Length
- **Caption length:** 80-150 words ideal. Can go longer for storytelling posts.
- **First line:** Visible without "more" — make it count (125 chars)
- **Hashtags:** 5-15, either at the end of caption or in first comment

## Content Types

### Photo + Caption
- Caption should complement the image, not describe it
- The photo stops the scroll; the caption delivers the value
- If the image is text-heavy (quote card), keep caption shorter

### Carousel (Multiple Images)
- Highest engagement format on Instagram
- Slide 1: Hook (title card)
- Slides 2-9: Content (one point per slide)
- Final slide: CTA or summary
- Design tip: consistent style, readable text, brand colors

### Reels
- Short-form video (15-90 seconds)
- Caption is secondary but still matters for SEO
- Include relevant keywords in caption

### Stories
- Ephemeral (24h), more casual
- Polls, questions, quizzes drive engagement
- Good for behind-the-scenes, quick updates

## Caption Structure

### Hook (First Line)
Must earn the "more" tap. Patterns:
- Question: "Ever wondered why some apps feel effortless?"
- Bold statement: "Your morning routine is killing your productivity."
- "The thing nobody tells you about [topic]"

### Body
- Short paragraphs or single-line breaks
- Emoji as bullet points is more accepted here than other platforms (but don't overdo it)
- Storytelling works well — mini narratives
- Include a value takeaway

### Visual Suggestion
Always include a "Suggested visual:" line after the caption. Describe what kind of
image or graphic would pair well — e.g., "Suggested visual: clean screenshot of the
feature in action" or "Suggested visual: text overlay quote card on dark background".
This helps users who don't have a content designer.

### Close
- Soft CTA: "Save this for later" / "Tag someone who needs this"
- Questions: "Which one resonates with you?"
- Don't: "Link in bio!" on every post

## Hashtags Strategy
- Mix of sizes: 5 large (500K+ posts), 5 medium (50K-500K), 5 small (10K-50K)
- Relevant > popular — #SmallBusinessTips beats #Love
- Place at end of caption or in first comment (both work)
- Create a few "hashtag sets" for different content types and rotate

## Tone
- More personal and visual than LinkedIn
- Authenticity > polish
- Emoji usage: moderate, purposeful (not every line)
- Can be more casual and "real"

## What to Avoid
- Stock photo energy — everything should feel genuine
- Walls of hashtags with no caption substance
- Copying LinkedIn posts verbatim (different audience, different vibe)
- Clickbait hooks with no payoff
- Posting without an image/visual strategy
