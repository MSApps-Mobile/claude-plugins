# Facebook Content Guide

## Format & Length
- **Ideal length:** 100-200 words
- **Character limit:** 63,206 (you won't need it)
- **Truncation:** ~477 characters before "See more"

## Tone & Style
Facebook is the most conversational platform. The audience expects to feel like they're
reading a post from a friend or peer, not a press release.

- Write like you're talking to a smart friend
- Warmth and relatability matter more here than on LinkedIn
- Sharing personal context is more welcome (but still purposeful)
- Community-building language works: "Has anyone else noticed..." / "I'd love your input on..."

## Content That Works

### Shareable Insights
Content people want to share with their own network. Frame for broad relevance.
> "The best advice I ever got about hiring: Don't hire for skills you can teach. Hire for instincts you can't."

### Discussion Starters
Genuine questions that tap into shared experience.
> "Small business owners — what's the one tool you'd never give up? Not the trendy one. The one you actually use every day."

### Behind-the-Scenes
More accepted on Facebook than LinkedIn. Real moments, real challenges.

### Event/Milestone Posts
Facebook's algorithm boosts life events and milestones. Use for launches, anniversaries, wins.

## Hashtags
- 0-2 maximum. Hashtags are not a major discovery mechanism on Facebook.
- Only use if there's a specific community hashtag relevant to the post.

## Link Sharing
- Facebook previews shared links with an image and title
- You can add commentary above the link
- Native content (text/photo) outperforms link posts in reach
- Alternative: share the insight as a post, put the link in comments

## Emoji
- Light usage is natural on Facebook
- Don't overdo it — 1-3 per post is fine
- Can use as visual separators in longer posts

## What to Avoid
- Corporate voice — feels out of place on Facebook
- Hard-sell promotional posts without value
- Excessive cross-posting from LinkedIn without adaptation
- Ignoring comments — Facebook rewards conversation
- Posting just links without commentary
