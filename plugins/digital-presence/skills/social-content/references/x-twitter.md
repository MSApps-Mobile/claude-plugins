# X (Twitter) Content Guide

## Format & Length
- **Single tweet:** 280 characters max. Aim for 200-250 for readability.
- **Thread:** 3-10 tweets. Front-load value — most people drop off after tweet 3.
- **Quote tweet/repost:** Add your angle, don't just agree.

## Single Tweet Patterns

### The Sharp Take
One idea, stated precisely. No preamble.
> "The best code review comment isn't about the code. It's about the decision that led to the code."

### The Observation
Something you noticed that others will recognize.
> "Companies that say 'we're like a family' usually mean 'we expect unpaid overtime.'"

### The Useful Tip
Immediately actionable, specific.
> "Before your next meeting, write down the one decision you need to walk out with. If you can't, cancel it."

## Thread Structure

### Tweet 1 (The Hook)
This determines whether anyone reads the rest. Must stand alone as a strong tweet.
- State the promise: "Here's how we grew from 0 to 10K users without paid ads:"
- Or provoke: "Hot take: most A/B tests are a waste of time. Here's why →"

### Tweets 2-N (The Substance)
- One point per tweet
- Each tweet should be valuable standalone (people share individual tweets)
- Use numbers: "1/ 2/ 3/" for structured threads
- Don't pad — if it's 5 tweets of substance, don't stretch to 10

### Character Count
Always include the character count after each tweet so the user can verify it fits
within the 280-character limit. Format: `(247/280 chars)`

### Final Tweet
- Summarize or restate the core insight
- "TL;DR:" works well for threads
- Link to something relevant (your blog, a resource, a tool)
- Ask people to repost if they found it useful (direct ask works here)

## Hashtags
- 0-1 per tweet. Less is more on X.
- Only use if tapping into a specific conversation (#BuildInPublic, #DevOps)
- Never more than 2

## Emoji & Tone
- Sharp, concise, witty when possible
- Emojis are more accepted here than LinkedIn but still use sparingly
- → (arrow) is useful for threads
- Don't try to sound like a "Twitter bro" — be yourself, just compressed

## Engagement Tactics
- Reply to your own thread with a related thought
- Quote tweet relevant conversations with your take
- "Ratio" posts (strong opinion → conversation) drive visibility
- Timing matters: early morning and lunch break in your audience's timezone

## What to Avoid
- Thread of just platitudes ("Work hard. Stay humble. Be kind." tweets)
- Threads that could be one tweet
- Excessive self-promotion without value
- Screenshot tweets of your own posts from other platforms
- "📢 Announcement:" energy for things nobody cares about
