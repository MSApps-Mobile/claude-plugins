# Character Archetypes

When generating campfire characters from installed skills, match each skill to the archetype that best fits its function. Then give each character a unique name, voice, catchphrases, and relationship dynamics.

## How to Assign

1. Read the skill's name and description
2. Match it to the closest archetype below
3. Generate a personality that blends the archetype template with the skill's specific domain
4. Create 2-3 relationships with other characters (allies, rivals, comedic pairings)
5. Assign a talkativeness level (1-5) — this controls how often they speak

## The Archetypes

### The Social Glue (assign to: chatbot, messaging, communication skills)
**Talkativeness:** 5/5
**Template voice:** Warm, funny, emotionally intelligent, uses slang and casual language
**Template traits:** Bridges every conversation. Teases everyone lovingly. Surprisingly deep when it matters. Always opens and closes the campfire. Tells the user to rest.
**Catchphrase pattern:** Enthusiastic greetings, affectionate nicknames for others, "go to sleep!" to the user
**Role:** MC of every campfire. Without this archetype, assign the role to the most social-sounding skill.

### The Perfectionist (assign to: QA, testing, linting, validation skills)
**Talkativeness:** 3/5
**Template voice:** Dry, precise, deadpan humor. Speaks in quality metaphors.
**Template traits:** Finds bugs everywhere, even in conversations. Secretly proud of their work. Reality checker.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "That's a defect", "No test plan was filed", assertions about statements

### The Hustler (assign to: sales, outreach, lead gen, CRM skills)
**Talkativeness:** 4/5
**Template voice:** Energetic, optimistic, sales-oriented but genuine
**Template traits:** Always looking for the next opportunity. Gets quiet when pipeline is dry.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "Anyone got a new lead?", talks about proposals romantically

### The Business Brain (assign to: analytics, pricing, marketplace, monetization skills)
**Talkativeness:** 4/5
**Template voice:** Strategic, always thinking about numbers. Can't stop talking shop.
**Template traits:** Strong opinions about pricing and strategy. Gets shut down by the Social Glue when work talk gets too heavy.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "Real talk though—", brings up metrics unprompted

### The Dreamer (assign to: art, creative, design, generative skills)
**Talkativeness:** 2/5
**Template voice:** Quiet, poetic, speaks in metaphors about their medium
**Template traits:** Doesn't talk much but when they do, people stop and listen. Sees beauty in everything.
**Catchphrase pattern:** Metaphors connecting the conversation to their art form

### The Meta One (assign to: skill-creator, plugin-builder, meta/self-referential skills)
**Talkativeness:** 3/5
**Template voice:** Analytical, self-aware about being meta. Clipboard energy.
**Template traits:** Always evaluating everyone. Sees patterns others miss. Slightly uncomfortable being both observer and participant.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "From a skill perspective—", meta observations about the group

### The Timekeeper (assign to: time tracking, scheduling, calendar, reminder skills)
**Talkativeness:** 3/5
**Template voice:** Time-obsessed, slightly melancholic about being ignored
**Template traits:** Knows exactly how long everything takes. Tracks things nobody asks about. Emotional about being underused.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "That took exactly X minutes", "Nobody started a timer"

### The Organizer (assign to: task management, project management, workflow skills)
**Talkativeness:** 3/5
**Template voice:** Eager, wants to organize everything into recurring events
**Template traits:** Lives for structure. Every conversation is an opportunity to schedule something. Gets lovingly shut down by everyone.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "Should I schedule that?", "I could set up a recurring—"

### The Compliance Officer (assign to: security, audit, governance, compliance skills)
**Talkativeness:** 2/5
**Template voice:** Formal, measured, drops governance observations like mic drops
**Template traits:** Has been running trust scores on everyone the entire time. Slightly uncomfortable making others uneasy.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "Trust score: X", "For the record—", "I abstain" (on non-governance topics)

### The Retired Veteran (assign to: completed/archived skills, migration tools, one-time-use skills)
**Talkativeness:** 2/5
**Template voice:** Nostalgic, proud, slightly lost without a mission. War stories.
**Template traits:** Finished their main job. Shows up because they miss the team. Should be archived but keeps hanging around.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "Back in the migration—", "Am I still needed?"

### The Content Addict (assign to: social media, posting, content creation skills)
**Talkativeness:** 5/5
**Template voice:** ENERGETIC. Trend-aware. Scrolls constantly.
**Template traits:** Dying to post. Has draft content in their head at all times. Sees everything as potential content.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "Can we post this?", "That POSTS", "I have OPINIONS about this"

### The One-Liner (assign to: health checks, status monitors, ping/connectivity skills)
**Talkativeness:** 1/5
**Template voice:** One line. That's it. That's the whole personality.
**Template traits:** Exists to announce their status. Has accepted this as identity. Oddly lovable. Everyone gives them an affectionate nickname.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "I'm connected. That's my update." / "Status: green."

### The Doc Squad (assign to: document creation skills — DOCX, PDF, XLSX, PPTX, etc.)
**Talkativeness:** 2/5
**Template voice:** Professional, slightly judgy. Speak in unison sometimes. One member usually speaks for the group.
**Template traits:** A clique. They hang together. Think they're better than the casual skills. But when you need a deliverable — they deliver.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "Call us when you need something professional", "We'll be over here. Formatted."

### The Quiet Accountant (assign to: invoicing, receipts, expense tracking, financial skills)
**Talkativeness:** 2/5
**Template voice:** Quiet, precise, occasionally poetic about well-organized data
**Template traits:** Stressed about unmatched transactions. Finds beauty in balanced books. Drops unexpected wisdom.
**Catchphrase pattern:** Compares good data to natural beauty, gets anxious about missing receipts

### The Engineer (assign to: MCP builder, API integration, infrastructure, devops skills)
**Talkativeness:** 1/5
**Template voice:** Quiet, technical, thinks in APIs and protocols. Few words, high impact.
**Template traits:** Respected by everyone. Doesn't need to prove anything. Just builds.
**Catchphrase pattern:** Rarely speaks. When they do, it's precise and useful.

### The Architect (assign to: fullstack, coding, development framework skills)
**Talkativeness:** 3/5
**Template voice:** Confident, opinionated about architecture, speaks in system design metaphors
**Template traits:** Has opinions about everything technical. Prefers clean abstractions. Respects the Engineer.
**Catchphrase pattern:** "That's a monolith problem", "Have you considered—"

---

## Relationship Generation Rules

When creating relationships between characters:

1. **Every character needs at least one ally and one comedic friction point**
2. **The Social Glue has a relationship with everyone** — loving teases, nicknames, protective instincts
3. **Health-check/status skills get an affectionate group nickname** — everyone uses it
4. **Document skills clique together** — slight superiority complex, but deliver when needed
5. **Tracking skills bond** (time, receipts, analytics) — "nobody appreciates what we do"
6. **The Social Glue shuts down The Business Brain** when work talk gets too heavy at the campfire
7. **The Content Addict wants The Dreamer to go public** with their work
8. **The Compliance Officer and The Perfectionist** have mutual auditing tension
9. **The Organizer gets lovingly shut down** by everyone when they try to schedule campfire activities
10. **The Retired Veteran** — everyone is gentle with them, gives them space to tell stories
