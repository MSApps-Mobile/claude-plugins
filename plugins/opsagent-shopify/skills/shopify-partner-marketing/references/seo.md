# SEO for Shopify Partners

Shopify Partner SEO is *not* the same as merchant-storefront SEO. You're optimizing **your agency's website** to be found by merchants searching for help. Different intent, different keyword set, different content.

## The three pillars (Shopify Academy framing)

1. **Technical SEO** — Core Web Vitals, mobile-first indexing, structured data, crawlability, sitemap hygiene, fast LCP.
2. **On-page / content SEO** — topical authority through clusters of in-depth content for buyer-intent keywords.
3. **Off-page / links** — earned links from category-authority sites in your industry. The single biggest factor in competitive Shopify keywords.

All three must run together. Strong content with no links won't rank competitive terms; great links to a slow site will lose to a fast competitor.

## Keyword mapping for Shopify Partners

Group keywords by intent:

| Intent | Examples | Conversion likelihood |
|---|---|---|
| **Brand** | "msapps shopify", "opsagents agency" | Highest |
| **Service + Modifier** | "shopify plus migration agency", "headless shopify experts", "shopify accessibility audit" | High |
| **Geo + Service** | "shopify partner tel aviv", "shopify expert hebrew", "RTL shopify development" | High |
| **Vendor comparison** | "wix vs shopify for fashion", "magento to shopify migration" | Medium-high |
| **Technical how-to** | "how to migrate woocommerce to shopify", "shopify checkout extensibility tutorial" | Medium (but builds authority) |
| **Top-of-funnel education** | "what is shopify plus", "shopify pricing 2026" | Low conversion, high traffic |

Build content clusters around High and High-medium intent first. Don't write top-of-funnel until you have buyer-intent coverage.

## Topical authority

Search engines reward depth, not breadth. The pattern that works:

- Pick **one pillar topic** per cluster (e.g., "Shopify Plus migration").
- Publish a **comprehensive 3,000–5,000 word pillar page**.
- Surround it with **8–15 supporting articles** (e.g., "Plus migration timeline", "Plus migration cost", "Plus migration SEO checklist", "Plus migration for fashion DTC").
- Internally interlink everything.

Three to five strong clusters built right beat 100 thin posts.

## Content pattern for partner SEO

Every page targeting a buyer-intent keyword should include:

- **Clear H1 matching the search query.**
- **TL;DR at the top.** People scan first.
- **2026-current data.** Fresh stats build trust.
- **A specific framework or checklist** the merchant could actually use.
- **Case-study proof.** Numbers, names, before/after.
- **Author byline with E-E-A-T signals** (experience, expertise, authoritativeness, trust). Display Shopify Verified Skill badges in the byline area.
- **Strong CTA.** Free audit, sample assessment, or "talk to us".
- **Schema markup** — Article, Person, Organization, FAQ where applicable.

## Earned links — the differentiator

The Shopify Academy's blunt finding: **brands generating the most organic traffic invest in category-level content that earns links from authoritative sources.** A genuinely useful guide gets cited by industry publications, complementary brands, and educational resources over time. Earned links are the primary factor in domain authority, and domain authority is what unlocks rankings in competitive partner keywords.

Tactics that work for Shopify Partners:

- **Original research.** Survey 100 Shopify merchants on a topic, publish the data. Other sites will cite it.
- **Definitive guides.** "The 2026 Shopify Plus migration guide" with downloadable checklists.
- **Tools / calculators.** A free Plus-readiness audit, a CAC calculator. Linkable assets.
- **Guest posts on Shopify ecosystem sites.** Klaviyo blog, Gorgias blog, podcast partners.
- **Partner directory inclusion.** Roundups of "best Shopify agencies" — pitch yourself.
- **Speaking + podcasts.** Each appearance generates a linked show notes page.

Bad-pattern link tactics: directory spam, link exchanges, paid links — these hurt long-term.

## Technical SEO checklist

For your agency site:
- [ ] LCP under 2.5s on mobile.
- [ ] CLS under 0.1.
- [ ] Mobile-first responsive across all pages.
- [ ] HTTPS, no mixed content.
- [ ] XML sitemap submitted to Search Console.
- [ ] Robots.txt clean (no accidental disallow on important paths).
- [ ] Structured data validated for Article, Organization, BreadcrumbList.
- [ ] Image alt-text present, descriptive.
- [ ] Internal linking dense within clusters.
- [ ] No orphan pages.
- [ ] Hreflang tags if you serve multiple languages.

For RTL/Hebrew sites: add `lang="he"` and `dir="rtl"` correctly, hreflang to English mirror, and confirm Hebrew-language schema. (See `rtl-chat-fixer` skill for related guidance.)

## Voice and intent search (2026)

Search behavior is increasingly conversational. Optimize for:

- **Question-format queries** — write H2s as questions, answer in the first paragraph (Featured Snippet eligible).
- **Long-tail conversational** — "what's the best Shopify agency for fashion brands under $10M" beats "fashion shopify agency" for many segments.
- **AI-overview eligibility** — concise, well-structured answers with citation-worthy data tend to be lifted into Google's AI summaries. Source citations and definitive numbers help.

## Measuring partner SEO

Track quarterly:
- **Organic sessions** to high-intent pages.
- **Organic-attributed pipeline** ($ value of meetings booked from organic traffic).
- **Keyword rankings** for your top 30 buyer-intent terms.
- **Referring domains** (RDs) growth — pace > 3–5 new RDs per month is healthy.
- **Content velocity** — new pieces shipped per quarter.
- **CWV scores** — track LCP, CLS, INP across mobile + desktop.

Vanity to *avoid*: total traffic, total keyword count, social shares.

## Local SEO for geo-focused partners

If MSApps targets Israel/Hebrew-speaking merchants:
- Google Business Profile filled out, Hebrew + English.
- Localized landing page (`/he/`) with proper hreflang.
- Hebrew testimonials.
- Listings on local agency directories (e.g., Mako, Geektime mentions, local Shopify partner roundups).
- Local citations in Hebrew-language ecommerce blogs and podcasts.

## Anti-patterns

- **Writing for keywords, not buyers.** SEO content that ranks but doesn't convert is wasted effort.
- **Thin content.** 500-word posts on competitive topics don't rank in 2026.
- **Ignoring case studies as SEO content.** Case studies rank for "[merchant brand] case study" and convert higher than generic posts.
- **Copying competitor content.** Original POV beats rewritten content every time.
- **Skipping technical SEO.** A great content site that loads in 6s loses to a mediocre one that loads in 1s.
- **Treating SEO as a one-time project.** It's a compounding system. 12+ months of consistent work before you see real results.
