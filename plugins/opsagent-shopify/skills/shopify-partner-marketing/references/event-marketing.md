# Event marketing for Shopify Partners

Events convert at higher rates than any other partner channel — but only when measured and run with intent. The Academy treats events as a separate module because most partners do them poorly: show up, hand out swag, never follow up.

## The 5 event types relevant to Shopify Partners

1. **Shopify-hosted events.** Shopify Editions (online), Shopify Unite, Shopify Plus Connect (regional in-person), Commerce+. Highest leverage — pre-vetted merchant audiences.
2. **Industry trade shows.** NRF (retail), Shoptalk, eTail, IRCE, CommerceNext. Big audiences, big budgets, varied ICP fit.
3. **Vertical conferences.** Outdoor Retailer (outdoor brands), Cosmoprof (beauty), Sourcing at Magic (fashion). Tight ICP fit, smaller scale.
4. **Webinars.** Cheapest, most controllable. Run your own + co-host with app partners. Highest ROI when integrated into multi-touch campaigns.
5. **Local meetups + dinners.** Hosted intimate events with 8–25 prospects. Best for ABM 1:1 and 1:few plays.

## Pick the right event with this filter

Score each event on:

- **Audience fit** — % of attendees in your ICP. < 20% fit = skip.
- **Decision-makers present** — VPs and founders, not interns and SDRs.
- **Cost-per-meeting estimated** — total cost (booth, travel, swag, opportunity) ÷ realistic meetings booked.
- **Competitive intensity** — too many other agencies = noise.
- **Pre-event engagement support** — Does the event share attendee lists for outreach?
- **Content opportunity** — Can you speak, sponsor a panel, host a side event?

Run the math before committing. A $50k trade show that produces 5 meetings ≠ a $50k trade show that produces 50.

## The pre / during / post structure

The single biggest mistake partners make is treating events as standalone moments. Events convert when they're the *middle* of a multi-touch sequence.

### Pre-event (3–6 weeks out)
- Pull the attendee list (if available).
- Match it against ICP — flag dream accounts.
- Outbound to flagged accounts: "We'll be at NRF, want to grab coffee Tuesday at 3?"
- Pre-promote your speaking slot / booth / side event.
- Calendar-block meetings into time slots before the event opens.
- Build a custom landing page for event traffic.

### During event
- Stick to the calendar — don't drift.
- Capture every conversation in CRM same day (use a quick mobile note).
- Photograph the booth, content, attendees (with consent).
- Run the side event (dinner / breakfast / cocktail) — those meetings matter most.

### Post-event (within 5 business days)
- Send personalized follow-ups, *not* a mass blast. Reference specific conversation points.
- Send promised assets (audit, deck, intro to a customer).
- Schedule the next call with hot leads.
- Capture a public thank-you / recap on LinkedIn.
- Internal debrief: what worked, what didn't, was the unit economics positive?

## Webinars — the workhorse

For a Shopify Partner without a $250k events budget, webinars are the highest-ROI event format.

Pattern that converts:

1. **Specific, narrow topic.** "How DTC fashion brands hit Shopify Plus thresholds and migrate without losing SEO." Not "Shopify Plus 101."
2. **Co-host with an app partner.** Your audience + theirs = bigger pool.
3. **Promotion through 4 channels** for 3 weeks — email, LinkedIn, partner co-promo, paid retargeting.
4. **Tight format.** 30 min content + 15 min Q&A. People drop after 45.
5. **Recorded + gated.** Drives downstream lead capture from those who couldn't attend live.
6. **CTA at minute 25.** Audit / consult / next webinar.
7. **Post-event nurture sequence.** 3-email follow-up.

Realistic numbers: 100–300 registrants, 30–40% attendance, 5–10% take the CTA, 10–20% of CTA-takers turn into pipeline.

## Sponsorship ROI — the math

Sponsorship is the most-misused partner channel. The simplest ROI formula:

```
ROI = (pipeline_generated_attributable_to_event × close_rate × deal_size) - total_event_cost
```

`total_event_cost` includes: sponsorship fee, booth, travel, hotel, swag, opportunity cost of the team being there instead of doing other work.

Track multi-touch attribution — give events partial credit for deals they touched, not just deals they originated. A common Academy answer pattern: an event might be the *3rd* touch in a 7-touch sequence, so a linear or U-shaped attribution model gives it 14–25% of revenue credit, not 100% and not 0%.

## Event KPIs (memorize)

Two clusters:

**Lead-gen / pre-conversion:**
- Booth scans / business cards collected
- Meetings booked
- Side-event attendees
- Content downloads from event landing page
- Social mentions / impressions

**Pipeline / revenue:**
- # named accounts engaged
- $ pipeline created
- $ pipeline accelerated (existing deals advanced)
- $ closed-won attributed (multi-touch)
- Payback period (months to recoup cost from closed revenue)

A trade show with 500 scans and 0 booked meetings is a fail.

## Speaking opportunities

Talks at events are higher leverage than booths because:
- Authority signal — speaker > sponsor.
- Audience self-selects to your topic = pre-qualified.
- Recording lives forever as content.

To pitch a speaking slot:
- Submit early (most CFPs close 4–6 months out).
- Bring a specific data-rich talk, not "thought leadership".
- Co-pitch with a merchant — case study talks beat solo agency talks.
- Lead with a proven topic from your blog/podcast that already has audience traction.

## Anti-patterns

- **Going to an event just because competitors went.** Run the cost-per-meeting math first.
- **Booth-and-wait.** Without a calendar of pre-booked meetings, ROI tanks.
- **No follow-up plan.** 50% of partners fail here — meeting captured, never converted.
- **Vanity sponsorships.** A logo on a banner with no booth and no speaker = wasted spend.
- **Skipping side events.** The dinner-the-night-before often produces more pipeline than the show floor.
- **No attribution model.** Without one, you can't justify event spend in next year's budget.

## Output checklist

- [ ] Event mix balances Shopify-hosted, industry, vertical, webinar, and meetup formats.
- [ ] Each event scored on audience-fit, decision-maker presence, cost-per-meeting.
- [ ] Pre / during / post motion defined per event.
- [ ] Side event (dinner / breakfast) scheduled when sponsoring big shows.
- [ ] Webinar program has a 4-week-out promotion plan.
- [ ] KPIs include both lead-gen and pipeline metrics.
- [ ] Multi-touch attribution applied to event credit.
- [ ] Stop rule defined: if cost-per-pipeline > X, drop the event next year.
