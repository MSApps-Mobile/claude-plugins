# Finding your audience — ICP, segments, and personas

The Academy is strict about a distinction many partners blur: **ICP ≠ persona ≠ target audience**. Get the vocabulary right; exam questions test it.

| Term | Describes | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Target audience** | The broad market you could conceivably serve | "DTC merchants on Shopify in North America" |
| **Segment** | A slice of that audience grouped by shared traits | "$1–10M GMV fashion brands" |
| **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)** | The best-fit *account* (firmographic, technographic, behavioral) | "Fashion DTC, $2–10M GMV, 50–500 SKUs, on Shopify Standard considering Plus, US/EU, growing 30%+ YoY" |
| **Buyer persona** | The *human* you sell to inside that account | "Daniela, VP of Ecommerce, 33, KPI'd on revenue/session, reads 2PM" |

You need all three. They build on each other: target audience → segments → ICP within a segment → personas within the ICP.

## Building an ICP — the 5–7 attribute rule

A strong ICP has **5–7 attributes** — fewer is too vague, more is unfocused. Pick from:

**Firmographic**
- Industry / vertical
- GMV or revenue band
- Company size (employees)
- Years operating
- Funding stage (DTC: bootstrapped vs. raised)
- Geography

**Technographic**
- Current platform (Shopify Standard, Plus, WooCommerce, Magento, custom)
- Stack signals (Klaviyo, Recharge, Gorgias, etc. — the tech stack often predicts the buyer's sophistication)
- Headless/Hydrogen vs. classic theme

**Behavioral / situational**
- Growth rate (e.g., 30%+ YoY)
- Trigger event (re-platforming, new round of funding, new market entry, holiday season)
- Pain signals (slow checkout, no email program, expanding internationally)
- Decision authority (founder-led vs. team-led)
- Buying cycle length

**Strategic fit**
- LTV potential (will they stay 5+ years?)
- Referral potential (will their friends buy?)
- Case-study potential (will their brand impress the next merchant?)

## Limit to 2–3 ICPs maximum

The Shopify Academy and outside ICP literature both insist: **more than 3 ICPs means you haven't focused enough**. Each ICP must have distinct motivations, distinct objections, and a distinct go-to-market motion.

For OpsAgents/MSApps, that probably means:
1. **DTC fashion / lifestyle, $2–10M, on Standard, considering Plus** — the migration ICP.
2. **B2B / wholesale brands moving to Shopify B2B from custom or Magento** — the modernization ICP.
3. *(Optional 3rd)* **Hebrew/RTL merchants in Israel needing localization** — the geo ICP.

## Personas inside an ICP

Each ICP carries 1–3 personas. Per ICP-1 above:

- **Daniela — VP Ecommerce.** Owns conversion, AOV, retention. Wants migration without a holiday outage. Reads 2PM, listens to *Lenny's Newsletter*, lurks in Online Geniuses.
- **Mark — Head of Engineering.** Owns the stack. Wants the migration to not break inventory sync. Reads dev.to, follows Hydrogen launches.
- **Founder.** Cares about speed and brand. Will green-light if the team trusts the agency. Reads Shopify Plus Year-in-Review.

Each persona needs: name, title, KPIs they're measured on, top 3 pains, info diet (where they get information), buying authority, and one objection they'll raise.

## How to find your audience (research methods)

The Academy walks through three:

1. **Mine your wins.** Look at your last 10 closed-won merchants. What attributes do they share? That's your real ICP, regardless of who you *thought* you targeted.
2. **Mine your losses.** Look at the last 10 closed-lost. What killed the deal? That's where your ICP definition is too loose.
3. **Talk to merchants.** 5–10 short calls with current and lost-deal merchants beats any survey. Listen for the verbatim language they use to describe their pain — that's your campaign copy later.

Shopify-specific data sources:

- **Shopify Partner Dashboard.** Shows the merchants you've referred — their plans, their growth, their churn. That's gold.
- **BuiltWith / Wappalyzer.** Tech-stack signals to find merchants matching your technographic ICP.
- **Apps directory.** Merchants using Klaviyo or Recharge cluster with specific buyer behavior — useful for outbound list-building.
- **Shopify Plus customer stories.** Public case studies tell you which agencies are servicing which verticals — both for inspiration and to identify white-space.

## Segmentation pitfalls

- **Demographic segmentation alone.** Age + geography ≠ a segment for B2B partner sales. Add behavioral or trigger data.
- **Static segments.** ICPs drift. Re-validate every 6–12 months.
- **Confusing high-volume with high-value.** A segment may have lots of merchants and tiny LTV. Map both axes.
- **"Anyone on Shopify" as a segment.** Not a segment.

## Output: the ICP one-pager

A complete ICP document looks like this (1 page max):

```
ICP-1: DTC Fashion, $2–10M GMV, Migration-Ready
Industry: Apparel & accessories DTC
Revenue: $2M–$10M GMV/year
Size: 5–25 employees
Tech: Shopify Standard, Klaviyo, Recharge ~50% of time
Trigger: Hit Shopify Standard's checkout limits, board pushing Plus
Geography: US (60%), EU (30%), Other (10%)
Growth: 30%+ YoY, 2+ years old
Authority: Founder + VP Ecom co-decide
Cycle: 30–60 day buying cycle
LTV: $80k year-1 services + 20% rev-share thereafter
Avoid: <$1M GMV (no Plus need), Custom platforms (different motion)
Personas: Daniela (VP Ecom), Mark (Head of Eng), Founder
```

If your team can't fill this in for each of your 2–3 ICPs, your audience is undefined and downstream campaigns will scatter.

## Output checklist

- [ ] Target audience → segments → ICPs → personas, each named, each distinct.
- [ ] 5–7 attributes per ICP (no more, no fewer).
- [ ] At most 3 ICPs total.
- [ ] Each ICP has 1–3 named personas with KPIs, pains, info diet.
- [ ] Each ICP has a *trigger* (a moment-in-time signal) that says "outreach now".
- [ ] ICPs validated against last 10 wins + 10 losses, not invented from a whiteboard.
