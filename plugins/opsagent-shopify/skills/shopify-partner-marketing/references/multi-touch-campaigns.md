# Designing a multi-touch campaign

A campaign is not a channel. A campaign is **a coordinated set of touches across multiple channels, aimed at a defined audience, around a single message and CTA, in a defined window, measured by a single primary KPI.**

Single-channel, single-touch outreach is *not* a campaign and won't be treated as one in the exam.

## The 6 elements of every campaign

1. **Goal** — a measurable business outcome (booked meetings, qualified pipeline, pre-launch sign-ups). One goal per campaign.
2. **Audience** — a single ICP × persona slice (not "all merchants").
3. **Offer / message** — one job-to-be-done, one hook, one promise.
4. **Channels** — at least 2, ideally 3–4. Each plays a distinct role.
5. **Sequence / timeline** — touches over a defined window (typically 4–8 weeks).
6. **KPI + attribution** — what you'll measure and how you'll credit each channel.

If any of these is missing, it's not a campaign — it's an ad-hoc tactic.

## Channel roles in a multi-touch sequence

Channels play different roles. Mix them by purpose, not by what's trending.

| Role | Best channels | Touch position |
|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | LinkedIn organic, content, podcasts, paid display | First touch, top-of-funnel |
| **Consideration** | Email nurture, retargeting, webinars, case studies | Middle touches |
| **Conversion** | Direct outbound (email/LinkedIn/WhatsApp), demo requests, sales calls | Late touches |
| **Trust signaling** | Verified Skill badges, partner directory, reviews, certifications | Throughout |
| **Re-engagement** | Lifecycle email, retargeting, event invites | After 30 days no response |

A common Academy pattern is **3-3-3**: 3 awareness touches → 3 consideration → 3 conversion. Each layer uses different channels.

## A worked example — "Plus Migration Ready" campaign

**Goal:** Book 20 discovery calls with $2–10M fashion DTC merchants in 6 weeks.
**Audience:** ICP-1 (Daniela persona).
**Message:** "Migrate to Shopify Plus in 6 weeks without losing a single conversion-rate point."
**KPI:** Discovery calls booked. Secondary: pipeline value.

| Week | Touch | Channel | Asset |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Awareness post | LinkedIn organic | Founder's POV: "What we learned migrating 30 stores to Plus" |
| 1 | Targeted ad | LinkedIn ads | Same content, ICP-1 targeting |
| 2 | Email 1 | Cold outbound | "Saw you're on Shopify Standard — quick observation" |
| 2 | Connection request | LinkedIn | Personalized to recent post |
| 3 | Webinar invite | Email | "Plus migration: 6-week playbook" — live + recording |
| 3 | Retargeting | LinkedIn ads | Webinar reminder to landing page visitors |
| 4 | Webinar | Live event | 30 min content + 15 min Q&A + 15 min CTA |
| 4 | Email 2 | Follow-up | Recording + offer of audit |
| 5 | Direct call | Phone / WhatsApp | High-intent attendees only |
| 5 | Case study email | Lifecycle | Brand X migration story |
| 6 | Last-chance email | Direct | "Closing the cohort — book by Friday" |

11 touches, 4 channels, one CTA path (audit → discovery call → proposal). That's a campaign.

## Frequency and cadence

- **Awareness touches:** 2–4 per week, distributed across channels.
- **Direct touches:** No more than 1 per channel per 3 days. Combine across channels (LinkedIn DM Monday + email Wednesday + LinkedIn comment Friday).
- **Drop-off rule:** If no response after 5 direct touches, move to nurture for 30 days, then re-attempt.

## Channel-prioritization framework (Shopify's intent × competition matrix)

When picking channels:

- **High intent + low competition** → **prioritize**. (e.g., niche newsletters, partner co-marketing with complementary apps)
- **High intent + high competition** → **test carefully**, watch CAC. (e.g., LinkedIn ads to "Shopify Plus" job titles)
- **Low intent + low cost** → **scale if profitable**. (e.g., SEO content, organic social)
- **Low intent + high cost** → **minimize or avoid**. (e.g., generic display, untargeted podcasts)

This is a high-probability exam question. Memorize the four cells.

## CTAs — one per campaign, sequenced

Every touch funnels to **the same CTA** (or a single chain of CTAs). The sequence is:

1. Soft CTA — "read this", "watch this", "follow us".
2. Medium CTA — "join the webinar", "subscribe", "DM me".
3. Hard CTA — "book a call", "request a quote", "start a project".

A campaign that mixes CTAs ("read this" → "buy now") confuses the audience. Stay on one ladder.

## Personalization tiers

- **1:many** — segment-level personalization (industry, plan tier).
- **1:few** — cluster of accounts with shared traits (e.g., "5 DTC Israeli fashion brands hitting Plus thresholds").
- **1:1** — bespoke per account. Reserved for high-value targets.

Match personalization tier to deal size. Don't 1:1 a $5k website refresh — bad unit economics. Don't 1:many a $250k Plus migration — converts poorly.

## Output: the campaign brief template

Every campaign should fit on one page:

```
Name: <campaign name>
Goal: <measurable outcome>
Window: <start - end>
Audience: <ICP × persona × trigger>
Offer / hook: <one sentence>
Primary CTA: <single CTA>
Primary KPI: <single number>
Secondary KPIs: <2-3 supporting>
Channels (with role):
  - Awareness: <channel + asset>
  - Consideration: <channel + asset>
  - Conversion: <channel + asset>
Sequence: <touches by week>
Attribution: <linear, U-shaped, time-decay, custom>
Owner: <name>
Budget: <USD>
Stop rule: <when to kill it>
```

## Anti-patterns

- **Channel-roulette campaigns.** "Let's try TikTok this month, Reddit next month." Not a campaign.
- **Spray-and-pray.** Same message to 1,000 random merchants. Audience missing.
- **Campaigns with no stop rule.** "We'll see how it goes" = will run forever, drain budget, never get evaluated.
- **Skipping middle-of-funnel.** Going from a LinkedIn ad straight to "book a call" with no nurture. Conversion will be terrible.
- **Forgetting the partner-program co-marketing kit.** Many of Shopify's official assets are pre-approved for partner use — they save hours and lend credibility.

## Output checklist

- [ ] One goal, one audience, one CTA-ladder.
- [ ] At least 2 channels, ideally 3–4.
- [ ] At least 5 touches across 4–8 weeks.
- [ ] Touches mapped to roles (awareness / consideration / conversion).
- [ ] Channels chosen by intent × competition × cost.
- [ ] Personalization tier matches deal size.
- [ ] KPI tied to revenue.
- [ ] Stop rule defined.
