# Positioning your Shopify partnership

The first decision a Shopify Partner makes — long before tactics — is **what kind of partner are you?** Shopify's three lanes are:

- **Build** — themes, custom storefronts, Hydrogen, Liquid, integrations.
- **Earn** — apps in the Shopify App Store, recurring revenue from extensions.
- **Grow** — services that grow merchants: design, dev, marketing, operations.

Most partners blend two. MSApps/OpsAgents is *Grow + Build*. Pick a primary and a secondary; trying to do all three with the same team produces blurry positioning and weak conversion.

## The positioning statement

Use this template:

> For **<ICP>** who **<job-to-be-done>**, our agency **<unique offer>**. Unlike **<closest alternative>**, we **<one differentiator>**.

Strong example:

> For **DTC fashion brands doing $2–10M GMV** who **need to migrate to Shopify Plus without losing SEO or conversion**, OpsAgents delivers **a 6-week migration with guaranteed parity in load time, Core Web Vitals, and revenue per session**. Unlike full-service Plus agencies, we **embed an AI ops layer that catches checkout regressions in real time**.

Weak example:

> "We're a full-service Shopify agency that helps brands grow online."

Why it's weak: no ICP, no specific outcome, no differentiator, no alternative referenced.

## The four levers of partner positioning

1. **Vertical** — fashion, food & bev, supplements, B2B, etc. Vertical specialization beats generalist 9 times out of 10 because referrals concentrate by industry.
2. **Stage** — pre-launch, $0–1M, $1–10M (most fragmented), $10M+ (Plus territory). Pick *one* primary stage.
3. **Service intensity** — done-for-you (high price, slow), done-with-you (medium), self-serve products (apps, courses, templates). Most agencies do one well.
4. **Geography / language** — local matters more than partners think. "Shopify Plus partner in Tel Aviv" or "RTL/Hebrew Shopify expert" beats "global agency" for the right merchant.

Pick *at most two* axes to differentiate on. Three creates a niche too small to feed the pipeline; one is usually too generic.

## Brand voice — Shopify's house style (mirror it)

When writing partner-facing or merchant-facing copy:

- **Plain language.** "Sell more online" not "leverage omnichannel paradigms".
- **Merchant-first.** Talk about *their* outcomes (revenue, conversion, retention), not your features (engagement model, methodology).
- **Optimistic but specific.** "You can launch in 6 weeks" beats "we're committed to your success."
- **Action-oriented headlines.** "Start selling on Shopify" not "About our approach to commerce."
- **Active voice.** "We migrated 200 stores" not "200 stores have been migrated by us."
- **Numbers earn trust.** "36% better checkout conversion vs. WooCommerce" beats "industry-leading conversion."

The Shopify Partner Marketing Toolkit (gated PDF in the course) supplies the official logo lockups, color guidelines, and approved language patterns. Use them — partner co-marketing material gets more reach when it visually matches Shopify.

## The "why Shopify" pitch (memorize it)

Every merchant conversation eventually asks "why Shopify and not [Wix / WooCommerce / BigCommerce / custom]?" The Shopify Academy answer:

1. **Conversion.** Shopify's checkout converts up to ~36% better than the next-largest commerce platform — that compounds across millions of sessions.
2. **Total cost of ownership.** SaaS hosting + first-party security + always-on updates beat self-hosted plus a maintenance bill.
3. **Ecosystem.** 13,000+ apps, 700,000+ partners, native first-party integrations.
4. **Path to scale.** Same platform from $0 to $1B+. Gymshark, Allbirds, Mattel all run Shopify Plus.
5. **Speed to launch.** A new store in days, not months. A Plus migration in weeks, not quarters.

Pick the 2 that match the merchant's actual pain. Don't recite all 5.

## What to put on your partner directory listing

Your Shopify Partners Directory listing is a marketing asset. Optimize:

- **Headline:** ICP + outcome (e.g., "Shopify Plus migrations for $2–10M DTC brands").
- **Services tags:** pick the tags that match your ICP's search behavior, not all of them.
- **Case studies:** 3–5 with merchant name, before/after numbers, and a quote.
- **Verified skill badges:** display every one your team has earned.
- **Languages:** if you serve non-English merchants, list every language — it's a discovery filter.
- **Pricing transparency:** ranges or starting-from prices help filter low-fit leads early.

Listings without case studies and badges get filtered out by sophisticated merchants in seconds.

## Sales pitch opening — Shopify Academy pattern

Open with the *pain*, not the *product*. The Academy teaches:

> **Pain → Stakes → Why Shopify → Why Us → Proof → CTA**

Example opening line for an outbound:

> "Most $2M fashion brands lose 20–30% of mobile sessions on the checkout page. We've migrated 30 of them to Shopify Plus and recovered an average of 14% revenue per session in 6 weeks. Worth a 20-min call?"

That sentence does all six steps. The exam will test whether you can identify the missing step in a sample pitch.

## Common positioning mistakes

- **"We're a Shopify agency."** Useless. Every agency is a Shopify agency now.
- **"We're affordable."** Race to the bottom. Compete on outcome, not price.
- **"We do everything."** Translates to "we don't specialize in anything you need."
- **Burying the badge.** If your team has Verified Skill badges, lead with them — they're the closest thing to social proof Shopify will mint for you.
- **Copy-pasting the merchant's language.** Reflecting their words is good in discovery; in your positioning you need *your* voice and a clear point of view.
