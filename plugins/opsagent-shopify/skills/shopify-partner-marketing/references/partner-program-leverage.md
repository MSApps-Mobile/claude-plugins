# Shopify Partner Program leverage

The Partner Program is itself a marketing channel. Most partners under-use it. This file is the cheat sheet of every program asset that a marketer can deploy.

## Program economics — memorize

- **Free to join.** No fee. Application takes minutes.
- **20% recurring commission** on every merchant referred, on their monthly subscription, indefinitely.
- **Markets Pro referral:** 1% of GMV through Markets Pro for 24 months, capped at $50,000 per referral.
- **Q1 2026 promo:** $500 USD per eligible new merchant referral, max 10 referrals = $5,000. Stacks on the 20%. Ends March 31, 2026.
- **Tiers:** Registered → Silver → Gold → Platinum. Two gating factors:
  - **Commercial Impact** — trailing-5-year revenue from referred merchants.
  - **Credential Attainment** — % of team with verified-skill badges.
  Both thresholds must be met to advance.
- **Verified Skill badges:** Earned by passing assessments; valid 2 years; shareable on Credly; counts toward Credential Attainment.
- **Free courses don't grant badges.** Only assessments do.
- **Some technical exams cost $99 per attempt.** Most marketing-track assessments are free.

## Partner Dashboard — your home base

Inside partners.shopify.com:

- **Referral tracking** — auto-credits revenue from merchants you refer.
- **Development stores** — unlimited free stores for client builds.
- **Affiliate links** — generate trackable referral links for any merchant signup flow.
- **Partner Academy** — courses + assessments.
- **Resource library** — sales decks, brand kits, co-marketing assets.
- **Partner directory listing** — your public profile.
- **App management** — if you publish apps.

Most partners log in once a month. Marketing should be in there weekly.

## Marketing assets Shopify gives partners (free)

The Partner Marketing Toolkit (gated PDF in the course) supplies:

- Logo lockups and badges.
- Brand color palette.
- Approved language patterns ("Powered by Shopify", "Shopify Partner", "Verified Skill").
- Email signature templates.
- Social asset templates.
- Sales pitch decks for end-merchants.
- Case-study templates.

Use them — they signal credibility *and* save design time.

## The directory listing as a marketing asset

Optimize aggressively:

- **Headline** = ICP + outcome (not "Shopify experts").
- **Service tags** match ICP search behavior (don't tag everything).
- **Languages** filter — list every one you serve.
- **Geographies** — be specific.
- **Case studies** — 3–5 with merchant names, before/after metrics.
- **Verified Skill badges** — display every one your team holds.
- **Pricing transparency** — ranges or starting-from prices filter low-fit leads.

Listings get *filtered* by sophisticated merchants. Listings without badges and case studies get cut from the consideration set in seconds.

## Co-marketing rights

Tier-2+ partners can co-author content with Shopify, get warm intros to enterprise prospects from the Shopify BD team, and get speaking slots at Shopify-hosted events. The path:

1. Hit tier requirements.
2. Build relationship with your Partner Manager.
3. Pitch a co-marketing concept (joint case study, joint webinar, vertical guide).
4. Execute and report results.

## App ecosystem partnerships

Shopify Partners aren't only the agency directory — apps in the App Store are also partners. A massive marketing surface area for service partners is co-marketing with apps that serve your ICP:

- **Klaviyo** — email/SMS for DTC.
- **Recharge** — subscriptions.
- **Gorgias** — support.
- **Loop / Returnly** — returns.
- **Triple Whale / Northbeam** — analytics.

Joint plays: webinar, case study, integration tutorial, agency directory listing in their ecosystem. App-partner audiences convert at higher rates than cold outbound because the trust transfers.

## Verified Skill badges — the trust currency

Earning a badge does three things:

1. **Visible on directory listing** — merchants filter by it.
2. **Credly-shareable** — drop into LinkedIn, email signatures, byline blocks.
3. **Counts toward tier advancement** — Credential Attainment is half the tier-gate equation.

Pick the badges that match your ICP's expectations. If you target Plus migrations, you want Plus / Theme / Liquid badges. If you target marketing services, you want the Marketing Fundamentals badge plus Selling and Positioning Marketing Products. If you cover ad services, the Shopify Audiences and Marketing Automation badges weigh more.

## The "Marketing Your Shopify Services as a Partner" course in context

This particular course is part of a broader set of marketing-track learnings:

- **"Marketing Your Shopify Services as a Partner"** — agency self-marketing (this course).
- **"Marketing Fundamentals"** — for selling Shopify's marketing products to merchants.
- **"Selling and Positioning Shopify Marketing Products"** — assessment-track for the marketing-products selling motion.

The exam for "Marketing Your Shopify Services as a Partner" focuses on agency-side GTM, *not* on the Shop Campaigns / Audiences / Collabs product details (those are the Marketing Fundamentals exam scope). Don't confuse them.

## Partner-program-related anti-patterns

- **Treating the program as paperwork** — missing the marketing assets, the directory, the referral revenue.
- **Not earning badges** — leaving conversion on the table at directory and pitch level.
- **Forgetting referral economics in pricing** — your "true" project profitability includes the 20% recurring commission *years out*. A low-margin project to a long-running merchant pays back forever.
- **Ignoring the partner manager** — once you have one assigned, regular touch-ins drive co-marketing access.
- **Not co-marketing with apps** — leaves the easiest pipeline source on the table.
