# Digital marketing for Shopify Partners

This module sits between *channel choice* (covered in multi-touch-campaigns.md) and *SEO* (covered in seo.md). Here we cover the digital channels themselves: paid, organic social, email, lifecycle, content distribution, and partnerships.

## The five digital-marketing channels every Shopify Partner uses

1. **Owned content + SEO** — your blog, case studies, and tutorial library, ranking on intent keywords. (Detail: `seo.md`.)
2. **Email** — newsletter, lifecycle, and outbound sequences.
3. **Organic social** — primarily LinkedIn for B2B partners; some Twitter/X, YouTube, TikTok depending on ICP.
4. **Paid acquisition** — LinkedIn ads, Google Search ads, retargeting, and (for some ICPs) Meta ads.
5. **Co-marketing and partnerships** — joint webinars, app-partner content, podcast tours, Shopify-led co-marketing.

Each plays a distinct role; mixing them well is the campaign job covered in `multi-touch-campaigns.md`.

## Paid vs. organic — the trade

| Lever | Speed | Cost | Halflife |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Paid** | Immediate | High | Stops the moment you stop paying |
| **Organic** | 3–9 months | Time + content cost | Compounds for years |

Most Shopify Partners under-invest in organic and over-rely on paid. Build the organic engine even if paid pays the bills today — paid CAC is rising; organic CAC trends down once you rank.

## LinkedIn for Shopify Partners — the deepest channel

For B2B Shopify Partner GTM, LinkedIn is unavoidable. Use it across three motions:

### Organic
- **Founder-led content.** Personal voice from the agency founder beats branded company posts 5–10x in reach.
- **Cadence.** 3–5 posts/week. Mix POV essays, case-study snippets, behind-the-scenes builds, and merchant tactics.
- **Engagement.** Respond to every comment for the first 90 minutes — that's when the algorithm decides distribution.
- **Comment strategy.** Comment thoughtfully on 5–10 ICP-buyer posts per day; that puts your name in front of their network.

### Paid
- **Sponsored content** to ICP-fit accounts (use job title + industry filters).
- **InMail / Message ads** for direct outreach to named accounts (use sparingly, ABM-only).
- **Document ads** convert higher than image ads for B2B service buyers.
- **Retargeting** — keep budget for warm audiences (visited the site, watched a webinar, opened an email).

### Networking
- **Connect first, sell second.** Connection request with no pitch → useful comments for 2 weeks → only then a soft ask.
- **Use the Sales Navigator** if your ABM list justifies it.

## Email — the workhorse

Three email programs every Partner runs:

1. **Newsletter.** Weekly or bi-weekly. Best content goes here. Drives organic + drives nurture.
2. **Lifecycle / nurture.** Automated sequences: post-event follow-up, post-download nurture, re-engagement.
3. **Outbound / cold email.** Direct ICP outreach. Personalization is mandatory; 1:many cold blasts hurt sender reputation.

Standards to memorize:
- **Subject lines under 50 chars.**
- **Preview text mirrors subject** — don't waste the second hook.
- **One CTA per email.**
- **Personalize first 2 lines, generic rest is fine** — disproportionate impact on reply rate.
- **Send Tuesday–Thursday** for B2B; avoid Mondays and Fridays.
- **Mobile-first formatting.** 60%+ of emails open on mobile.

## Content distribution

A piece of content isn't done when it's published. Distribute every asset across:

- The blog (SEO).
- The newsletter.
- LinkedIn (carousel + text post + comments).
- Twitter/X (short threads).
- YouTube or Loom (video version).
- A podcast guest spot if it warrants depth.
- Email to your sales pipeline (sales-led distribution).

The Academy's rule: **if you spent 10 hours making it, spend 10 more distributing it.** Most partners flip that ratio.

## Co-marketing — the highest-leverage channel

The cheapest, most credible source of Shopify Partner pipeline is **other businesses already serving your ICP**. The plays:

- **App-partner co-marketing.** Klaviyo, Recharge, Gorgias, Loop, etc. each have partnership teams. Joint webinars + co-branded landing pages + shared lead lists.
- **Complementary-agency co-marketing.** Agencies that serve adjacent needs (CRO, design, ads) — share leads, run joint case studies.
- **Shopify-led co-marketing.** Higher-tier partners get warm intros, co-authored content, and event slots from Shopify itself.
- **Customer-advocate co-marketing.** Successful merchants will co-host webinars or join your podcast — both audiences win.

Co-marketing converts at higher rates than cold outreach because the audience is pre-warmed by the trusted intermediary.

## Paid search — when it's worth it

Google Search ads are *expensive* for partner keywords ("shopify plus agency", "shopify migration") because the bid auction is dominated by big agencies. Worth it only if:

- You're protecting your brand name from competitor bids.
- You target long-tail keywords with buyer intent ("shopify plus migration cost", "klaviyo to shopify integration consultant").
- You already have a strong landing page and case studies — generic landing pages waste paid traffic.

Default: deprioritize broad paid search. Reinvest in SEO + LinkedIn instead.

## Display and retargeting

- **Display** to cold audiences = mostly waste for a Shopify Partner. Skip.
- **Retargeting** = essential. Warm audiences (visited the site, opened an email, attended a webinar) convert at 5–10x cold.
- **Set up retargeting pixels day one.** LinkedIn Insight Tag + Meta Pixel + Google Tag.

## Channel-mix budget heuristic for a $5–25M Shopify Partner

Approximate annual marketing spend allocation:

- Content + SEO: 30–40%
- LinkedIn organic + paid: 20–30%
- Email tooling + lifecycle: 5–10%
- Events + co-marketing: 15–25%
- Brand + creative: 5–10%
- Retargeting: 5–10%
- Tools / attribution / analytics: 5%

Adjust by ICP and stage, but the ratios above sit close to what well-run partner agencies report.

## Tracking the funnel

Every digital channel must hand off to a CRM (HubSpot, Pipedrive, Salesforce, or even a tight Notion+spreadsheet for early-stage partners) so you can attribute pipeline and revenue back. Without that hand-off, every channel decision is a guess.

## Output checklist

- [ ] Channel mix justified by ICP behavior, not by trend.
- [ ] Paid + organic balance present (don't run only one).
- [ ] LinkedIn motion includes organic + paid + networking.
- [ ] Email program covers newsletter + lifecycle + outbound.
- [ ] Co-marketing plan with at least 2 named partners.
- [ ] Distribution multiplier applied (≥5 outlets per asset).
- [ ] Retargeting pixels live across all properties.
- [ ] Channel attribution wired to the CRM.
